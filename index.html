Before you blame the player, check the picture.

This section strengthens the emotional and practical message before the reader enters the full book.

1

Football uses colour everywhere

Kits, bibs, cones, balls, lines, goalkeeper shirts, referee shirts, tactical boards and coaching language can all become hidden colour tests.

2

Confusion can look like poor decisions

A player might seem slow, nervous or careless when they are actually checking, comparing, guessing and trying to avoid a colour mistake.

3

Clearer cues protect talent

Numbers, contrast, patterns, positioning, plain language and colour-safe design can reduce mental load without lowering football standards.
